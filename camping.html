Top Camping Sites in Sri Lanka

Are you and your spouse one of those crazy nature lovers looking for an adventurous holiday in Sri Lanka? You’ve come to the right spot! From rainforests to jungles to tea estates to beaches to riversides, you decide where you want to camp for the night! If you are wondering whether you might have to compromise on comfort and luxury to get hold of this adventurous holiday, don’t sweat it. We have couple camping options in Sri Lanka to suit your requirements; be it a restricted budget or a luxury honeymoon.

1. Belihuloya Caping Site

Belihul Oya is a unique climatically transitional area that connects the dry and wet zones of Sri Lanka and consists of moist semi-evergreen forests, montane temperature forests, tropical savanna forests and dry grasslands. Another highlight in Belihul Oya is a natural rock pool where two streams meet and create a natural bathing area with clear and unpolluted water. While at this one-of-a-kind campsite, feel free to try activities such as canoeing and trekking with your loved one. Best time to visit: Throughout the year Camping Companies: River Garden Resort – Semi luxury camping

2. Kithulgala Camping Site

Only for the true nature lovers: enjoy an adventure filled camping holiday in Kitulgala with your partner. Camping here means you get to wake up to the sounds of birds chirping and water flowing from nearby waterfalls. Kitulgala is the wettest zone in all of Sri Lanka and is the base for white water rafting. Hence you can partake in fun activities such as white water rafting, trekking, nature trails, bird watching, cycling, river expeditions, swimming in waterfalls, confidence jumps and much more. Best time to visit: January to April.

3. Veddha Village

To be absorbed in one of the oldest civilizations of the country, you should definitely try camping in the Dambana Veddha village of Sri Lanka. People here have their own cultures and traditions, so this will be very interesting because you can meet them and talk about their life styles. You can also enjoy their unique cuisines and know more about the history of Sri Lanka. Best time to visit is : Throughout the year

4. Kalpitiya Beach Camping

Like I mentioned there are many camping options in Sri Lanka, jungles and lakes are not the only place – you can camp in a beach too. This will be completely different, as you will be waking up to the sounds of the waves clashing against rocks with a beautiful sunrise in-front of you. Also Kalpitiya beach is one of the best beaches in Sri Lanka that offers you a wide range of activities like Canoeing, dolphin watching and kite surfing. Best time to visit is : October to May
